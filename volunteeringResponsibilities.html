Volunteering Responsibilities

Volunteers may be asked to work on a variety of tasks once they arrive at the pantry, including stocking shelves, greeting families, handing out food, and making bags of food. This is a great opportunity to interact with people in the Princeton community and give back to those in need. Please note that when you sign up, you are making a commitment to volunteer during the time you have signed up for, and not showing up or canceling at the last minute could have a negative impact on the operations of the food pantry. If you decide to cancel/reschedule, please do so at least 48 hours in advance so that someone else may sign up in your place. Take a look at the sections below for more information on how the volunteering process works!

Before You Arrive

Make sure that you have been successfully scheduled for a volunteering time slot before showing up. If you have email notifications activated on your account, you'll get a reminder before your time slot. If not, make sure to keep track of the time slots you registered for, and set alarms as necessary. When preparing to go to the food pantry, wear clothes that you don't have a problem getting dirty (which shouldn't be much of a problem).

Packing Family Bags

Based on the directions of another volunteer, you may be asked to pack food bags for different types of families. This involves putting the required number of the specified food products in each bag and making sure that you have the correct number of bags. Pay extra attention to this process, as you may forget which bags have already been taken care of if you're not careful.
